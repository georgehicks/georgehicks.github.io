The gospel is the good news of what God has done for us — and an invitation into a living, daily relationship with Him.

scroll
Part One

The Heart of the Gospel

God loved first.

"For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life."

John 3:16

"But God shows his love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."

Romans 5:8

"In this is love, not that we have loved God but that he loved us and sent his Son to be the propitiation for our sins."

1 John 4:10

"How much more will the heavenly Father give the Holy Spirit to those who ask him!"

Luke 11:13

"We love because he first loved us."

1 John 4:19

Jesus lived the perfect life we could never live, died on the cross to pay the penalty for our sins, rose from the dead on the third day, and now offers us forgiveness, new life, the gift of the Holy Spirit, and eternal relationship with the Father — all as a free gift received through faith.

This is the heart.

Part Two

The Heartbeat
of the Gospel

Once we have received this gift, God invites us into a living, daily relationship with Him.

The Entry Point

Faith

Without this, nothing else works.

pistis — the Greek word behind "faith" — is closer to what we'd call trust today: reliance, confidence, entrusting yourself to someone. Not intellectual assent. A living, relational lean into who He is.

"And without faith it is impossible to please him, for whoever would draw near to God must believe that he exists and that he rewards those who seek him."

Hebrews 11:6

The Daily Rhythm

Abide  →  Reach & Receive  →  Follow

Abide
The constant breath — staying connected in the eternal now with the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit

"I am the vine; you are the branches. Whoever abides in me and I in him, he it is that bears much fruit, for apart from me you can do nothing."

John 15:5

"If you abide in me, and my words abide in you, ask whatever you wish, and it will be done for you."

John 15:7

"Abide in me, and I in you. As the branch cannot bear fruit by itself, unless it abides in the vine, neither can you, unless you abide in me."

John 15:4

"As the Father has loved me, so have I loved you. Abide in my love."

John 15:9

→
Reach & Receive
The frequent drink — bringing everything to the Father, listening for His still small voice, receiving what He gives back

"The Son can do nothing of his own accord, but only what he sees the Father doing. For whatever the Father does, that the Son does likewise."

John 5:19

"Whatever you ask in prayer, believe that you have received it, and it will be yours."

Mark 11:24

"Ask, and it will be given to you; seek, and you will find; knock, and it will be opened to you."

Matthew 7:7

"And after the fire the sound of a low whisper."

1 Kings 19:12

→
Follow
The big sustaining meal — doing what we have heard and received, denying self, obeying His voice

"If anyone would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross and follow me."

Matthew 16:24

"My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me."

John 10:27

"If you love me, you will keep my commandments."

John 14:15

"But be doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving yourselves."

James 1:22

The Cost

The Cost of Full Faith

pistis — reliance, confidence, entrusting yourself to someone.
Jesus never hid the price. Lesser faith means lesser participation.

"For whoever would save his life will lose it, but whoever loses his life for my sake will find it."

Matthew 16:25

"So therefore, any one of you who does not renounce all that he has cannot be my disciple."

Luke 14:33

"If anyone would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross daily and follow me."

Luke 9:23

"If anyone comes to me and does not hate his own father and mother and wife and children and brothers and sisters, yes, and even his own life, he cannot be my disciple."

Luke 14:26

Part Four

The Two Operating Systems

The World's Way
Scarcity, self-powered, erosive
◆ Scarcity assumed
◆ Anxiety as steady tone
◆ Control & management
◆ Accumulation
◆ Self-protection & exclusion

"For to set the mind on the flesh is death, but to set the mind on the Spirit is life and peace."

Romans 8:6

"Do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewal of your mind…"

Romans 12:2

The Kingdom's Way
Abundance, generous, God-powered
◆ Sufficiency given
◆ Trust as steady tone
◆ Reception & participation
◆ Daily portion
◆ Generosity & inclusion

"The fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control."

Galatians 5:22–23

"I came that they may have life and have it abundantly."

John 10:10
